It was a very enjoyable cruise, however compared to one of their competitors, there are a few things I would rate them very low on. 1) Uniworld only accepts credit card payment. We do not carry a lot of credit cards and like to keep our limits very low, thus requiring the use of several credit cards. Their competitor allowed a direct payment from our bank account. This was much easier and simpler for us. I would prefer having the option as to whether or not I have to use my credit card. 2) We arrived on board the ship at 10:30 in the morning after flying all night but were not allowed into our room until 4 hrs. later. Consequently, my husband became over tired and was sick the entire cruise and was never able to participate in any of the shore excursions. I feel they should have advised us on making the cruise reservation the rooms would not be ready until 3 PM so we could have arranged our flight accordingly. Or, since they knew there were a few of us arriving earlier, have an area available for us to rest (actually lie down not sit in the lounge) or have our rooms ready earlier. 3) The drop off at the airport was very poor. Again, with the competitor, on the departure flight we were dropped at the departure at the front of the airport terminal and had someone there immediately to direct us to the correct ticket counter and make sure we got through security. Uniworld, the driver of the van merely pulled in dropped us off in a parking lot and unloaded our luggage. The driver did not even indicate where the door to the terminal was or what level we needed to go to. I had to ask where the door was and upon entering the terminal there was a very nice airline pilot who directed us to the proper level for check in.

The Belgium Christmas Markets as well as the tours in each city were planned and staffed by experienced locals who were keen to share their experiences and stories.
