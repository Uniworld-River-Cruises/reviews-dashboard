Decadent Danube trip Uniworld Boutique River Cruise October 2025 S S Beatrice is a beautifully presented ship, finished and kept to a high standard. Our cabin, called a Stateroom, was on deck 3 at the stern. Modest sized but adequate, with an opening window but no balcony. A neat shower in a compact bathroom. Every day the bedding and towels were changed, the room was cleaned, and toiletries were replaced as needed. There was no tea/coffee making facility. Large choice of food in the restaurant, buffet at breakfast and lunch and table service in the evening. Well furnished dining room with leather chairs. Good choice of wines, introduced by a witty female sommelier. The serving staff worked well together. Every morning Ingrid, the Cruise Manager, addressed the guests, with useful and comprehensive information about that day’s events. There was also a daily note left on our bed, showing the following day’s timetable. Sometimes we felt there were too many communications.: Excursions were mostly good with knowledgeable guides, particularly the first one, a young lady fresh from university, who was most entertaining. Some excursions were occasionally too rushed, particularly the Belvedere visit, where one small gallery was just walked through. One extended excursion on foot was to a cathedral with a male guide who walked very fast, ignoring the 80+ age of three ladies in the group. Extraordinarily, having walked around the outside of the vast cathedral, the group was not allowed to see inside the cathedral, even for a quick peek, the excuse being that the coach would be kept waiting. In the event, the group was kept waiting for the coach for over half an hour. Based on our experience, that guide let Uniworld down. There was a visit to hear a very good male voice choir, and an enjoyable afternoon Strauss/Mozart private concert at extra cost. Other excursions included a privately owned castle and an excellent boat museum. There were interesting and well presented talks about the route of the cruise and about the locks structures. On our last evening there was a fun performance from a top-rate female singer-comedian.l We were lucky with the weather - all sunny days except in Nuremberg on the day we returned home. Beautiful autumn colours on the river banks and little sign of industry. Now for the not-so-good. Departure from Heathrow was at 6am, so our small group stayed overnight but still had a disturbed night. We won’t do that again. Instead we will fly out a day or two earlier and take a hotel there. The flight back was easier but still involved a coach to Munich, the flight to Heathrow, and a taxi home - a lot of sitting down. As mentioned, our cabin was at the stern, over the engine room. I have good hearing and could not believe the noise the engines made at night, sometimes over 90dB. Simple plastic earplugs were no match for the engines. I tried to get a move to another cabin but the ship was absolutely full. In fairness, towards the end of the cruise the captain kept the engine speed down at night but my sleep was down to four to five hours a night, and I am usually a good sleeper. That means we will never to take a river cruise again without being assured of a cabin well away from serious noise. More good news: it was all inclusive, including gratuities.
