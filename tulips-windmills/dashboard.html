This was my fifth Uniworld cruise. As with them all, the service was outstanding, every staff person went out of their way to be helpful and pleasant. The excursions were all excellent; ironically there were so many, and so good, that for me there wasn't enough down time to relax! But there was a serious shortfall that would make me hesitate to take another Uniworld cruise. I am a vegetarian, something that I stated when I booked, and confirmed was in my record before the cruise began. But I found that the vegetarian choices were totally inadequate. Most of the lunch buffets had no hot vegetarian items containing any protein. Not even pizza or grilled cheese sandwiches. The mac and cheese served one day was the best thing they had in all 9 lunches. And while almost all the dinner courses included a vegetarian option, many were not very good. The best was veggie meatballs, they were so good (and other choices so inadequate), that I requested they serve them again to me. I also would not consider another cruise on the River Princess. Except for the suites, the cabins, even those with French balconies, were just 151 square feet, and this is frankly too small to be comfortable. The ship does not have a pool, and while for this sailing it would have been too cold to use a pool on the sun deck, it made me realize that in the future, I would only choose a ship with an indoor pool.
— Ellen, Tulips & Windmills | River Princess, River Duchess | ★★★★ | staff, food, excursions, accommodation, ship, service
The itinerary was very good. We were on the last sailing of the season, so we were prepared for not seeing tulips in all their glory...and we were right. We visited the tulip bulb farm and Keukenhof Gardens on the very last day of availability. The flowers were all being cut and removed the very next day. The flowers were not all in the greatest condition, but it was still wonderful to see. If this is important to you, I suggest that you make sure you book an earlier date. The cruise itinerary was actually changed so that we arrived in Haarlem earlier than originally scheduled; if it hadn't been changed, we would have missed it all. The staff onboard were all marvelous. The local guides were mostly very good. The guide we had in Nijmegen could have been better; we felt some of his comments were somewhat inappropriate. I don't remember his name, but he had a student with him, who he mostly dismissed throughout the entire tour. I did not enjoy the Fabrique des Lumiere experience. It dragged on too long for me, and I do experience motion sickness when trying to watch objects flitting around on screens or walls. I would have preferred a shorter presentation of the various artworks that didn't add animation to them. It made them appear cartoonish. We were in a deluxe stateroom (River Princess), which is the same size as the classic and french balcony rooms. I expected it to be small, but I did not expect that it would not be possible to physically separate the twin beds. They were made up as individual beds, but due to the furniture choices they could not be moved apart. Narrower (and more practical) side tables would have allowed space between the beds. Also, the bathroom was surprisingly cramped. I travelled with a friend, and we managed. But if my husband (6' 2", 260 pounds) had been with me, he would have had a very challenging time showering. And he definitely would not have been able to dry off in the bathroom. Our room was on Main deck, pretty much right at the water level. Many times throughout the cruise our window was blocked by the pier. I was again surprised at how many people with mobility challenges were on the ship. And then complained about the 'speed' of the walking tours. Europe is an old country, and was not built to North America's accessibility standards. None of the walking tours that we took could be considered fast-paced; we actually had trouble staying at the slow pace at which the guides were walking. I would stronger suggest that you consider abilities before committing to any river cruise. They just aren't designed for people who need walkers or wheelchairs. And on that note, The Netherlands is a bike-friendly country (as advertised). So much so, that tourists are at risk of being injured if they linger too long in a bike lane. And we (North Americans) are not used to dedicated bike lanes being where we are used to there being sidewalks. We were warned continuously throughout the cruise about the bicycle mentality of Amsterdam, and they did not exaggerate. So be prepared and aware. Overall, I very much enjoyed the cruise (the food, the staff, the entertainment, the excursions).
