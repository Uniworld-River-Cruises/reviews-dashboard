— Christine, Portraits of Eastern Europe | S.S. Beatrice | ★★★★ | staff, food, accommodation, ship
Firstly, the people from ship Captain on down, the food and wine, the opportunities to go on superb tours and the comfort of the ship were absolutely first rate. Uniworld has a first-rate team and this is reflected in the personal warmth, quality of service and comfort we experienced. This was a trip of two parts. Twenty something of us were on the trip for two weeks, while the remainder were on for only one: either Bucharest-Budapest, or Budapest-Prague. The two weeks were very different in tone, perhaps reflecting the less touristy aspects of the first week. The travelers in that week seemed more serious and interested in getting more deeply into what was available. The travelers in the second week seemed more of a middle-aged party crowd, and they were certainly focused more on drinking than the first week's group. Why not 5/5? A couple of niggles. One is that there were fewer in-depth lectures than we had hoped for. Romania, Bulgaria, Serbia, Croatia and Hungary have complex histories and it would have been welcome to have more substance covering them. Second, the tours could be a bit superficial. Ironically, the second week had what was probably the (optional) tour with the largest amount of serious content -- the visit to Terezin -- but justice could not be done in a 1/2-day tour. We wound up speeding through the museum in the Terezin ghetto in record time, without time to absorb a huge amount of material. We came away with a lot less than we could have. Please, seriously consider making this a full-day tour in the future. Third, we also found that several of the local guides, knowledgeable though they might be, had very thick accents when speaking in English. This might sound churlish, in that I cannot speak their native languages at all, but it did make it difficult to understand all they were saying. Fourth ... well, we are experienced travelers, and the second week in particular was a bit disappointing.

After complaining 4 or 5 times, they sent a engineer to our room to fix it. He did something to the toilet, but again, the sewer smell remained. In addition to my 6 complaints, I included an explanation of the situation on a survey that was left in our room sometime between Bucharest and Budapest. The survey request was signed by Hotel Manager, Alexandru Marinescu, so I thought he would be reading my comments. I turned the survey in to the Reception Desk and never heard back. The sixth time I complained to the Purser, I felt he disrespected me by his comment that I had complained enough and they could do nothing else about the smell. At that point, I argued that we had paid a lot of money for the cruise and should not have to put up with a sewer smell for 6 days. At this time, we were in Budapest and some of the crew were changed out. We had a new Hotel Manager. His name was Slaven Teofilovic, and he overheard my conversion with Purser Nenad Petrikonja. Mr. Teofilovic asked if he could help with something. I explained the situation to him and he apologized and said he would get it resolved immediately. My husband and I went back to our stateroom and shortly thereafter there was a knock on our door. It was a maintenance man and as soon as he entered our room, he knew right away it was the shower causing the sewer smell. He lifted up the the shower drain and there all kinds of black gunk there. We left the room and when we returned after dinner, we immediately noticed the sewer smell was gone. My question is why did they treat the toilet for six days when it was the shower drain all along? Why did I have to report the problem to the Purser for 6 days? Why didn't the daily housekeeping staff report the problem before we boarded the ship as the sewer smell was so strong? After the sewer smell was fixed, our air conditioning stopped working. We were told it was because the French balcony door was ajar. We made sure the door was tightly shut and locked, but it constantly unlocked by itself. Hotel Manager, Mr. Teofilovic, sent maintenance crew to our stateroom. Come to find out, the door was not the problem, it was actually the air conditioner. We had to leave the room for several hours while it was being fixed by two crew members. I don't know what took so long. They also left a lot of dust in the room as they had to take the air conditioning panel off on the counter where we had several items stored. There was even dust balls on the chair we used. They could have done a better job with the clean-up. I know this is a lot of information, but before I sign off, I want to compliment Hotel Manager, Slaven Teofilovic. He did an excellent job in resolving our issues expediantly once he boarded the ship in Budapest. Too bad he wasn't there the first 6 days of our cruise. — Michael, Portraits of Eastern Europe | S.S. Beatrice | ★★★ | food-quality, ship-condition, itinerary-changes, space-size, temperature, value
We had high expectations for our Uniworld River Cruise, but unfortunately, the experience fell well below what I was promised. The food onboard was consistently disappointing—bland, poorly presented, and lacking variety. For a cruise that markets itself as a luxury experience, the quality of the meals was simply unacceptable. The stateroom was another letdown. It was uncomfortably hot throughout the trip, and despite multiple complaints, the temperature issues were never resolved. This made it very difficult to relax or get a good night’s sleep. Even the common areas were not up to standard. The lounge chairs were hard, outdated, and uncomfortable—definitely not suited for long periods of sitting and relaxing, which is a big part of what you’d expect to do on a river cruise. Overall, this was not the premium experience I was expecting. Uniworld really needs to address these issues if they want to maintain their reputation as a luxury cruise provider. We will never recommend Uniworld nor ever be a return customer!
