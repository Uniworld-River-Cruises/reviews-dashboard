— Susan, Castles along the Rhine | S.S. Elisabeth, S.S. Antoinette, S.S. Victoria | ★★★ | food-quality, excursion-quality
This was our first river cruise, and it provided a contrast to our prior cruising experiences, which include a Royal Caribbean voyage to the Caribbean (two decades ago with young children), National Geographic Lindblad expeditions to the Galapagos, and Atlas Ocean Voyages to the Arctic and Antarctica. A notable aspect of our preferred travel experiences involves the educational enrichment provided by onboard subject matter experts. Regrettably, apart from a single afternoon's commentary on the castles along the Rhine, there was a noticeable absence of historical discourse or lectures concerning these significant landmarks, geography and political landscape. The S.S. Antoinette exhibits signs of wear. Our stateroom (422) featured a noticeable stain on the fabric wall, approximately four feet from the base, and the bathroom emitted a persistent musty odor. The cross-hatched lattice work within the restaurant appeared aged and soiled. The ship's overall aesthetic, despite its homage to the Antoinette theme, conveyed a dated, somber, and uninspired impression. Several fellow passengers, drawing upon their prior river cruise experiences, remarked that the Antoinette's layout was the least optimal they had encountered. The top deck is in need of refurbishment. Upon boarding, the brown carpet was saturated and remained damp for several days, despite minimal rainfall. Furthermore, the lounge chair covers were rarely fully removed, even on sunny days during the scenic journey past the castles. The cushions for the metal chairs were provided by the crew on only one or two occasions. At times, the atmosphere suggested a focus on alcoholic beverages, with abundant wine service at dinner and the two other social areas frequently occupied by groups engaged in extensive drinking. This environment did not align with our expectations for this type of tour. During a "black and white" themed dinner, the crew's disorganization and inattentiveness were such that a table-mate retrieved a bottle of wine from the buffet herself. A server, upon observing this, reprimanded the guest quite sternly, stating that their employment was jeopardized should any supervisor witness the action. This incident created a palpable sense of discomfort and intimidation among our group. We also feel that the itinerary, while visiting several villages and cities along the Rhine, gives the impression that Basel, Switzerland and Amsterdam, The Netherlands was part of the tour. This wasn’t the case as there were no activities planned at either port, they were just ports of embarkation and disembarkation, nothing more.
— Jonathan, Castles along the Rhine | S.S. Elisabeth, S.S. Antoinette, S.S. Victoria | ★★★ | food-quality, ship-condition, cleanliness, unmet-expectations
The excursions and guides were very good. I would have liked more time to explore each site. The two best tours were the Secret Garden and the evening castle tour. They felt unique and special. The meals that were served were very good, however the staff seemed short handed. Serving took a long time and getting attention for tea or coffee was sometimes very difficult. The SS Antoinette is in desperate need of a top to bottom refresh. My room smelled moldy and i complained about it several times. The wall paper had large water stains on the wall across from the bed. My room was 422. Everything felt dusty, musty and in need of a deep clean or replacement. The chairs on the top deck were covered. Guest were expected to uncover the chairs themselves, while this should have been done by the staff. The outdoor carpeting on the top deck held water. The day we boarded the ship the carpeting was soaked. I am not sure if it ever dried as I stopped going up there. Have you considered adding educational sessions about the history of the towns we visited or the local wildlife? There were no educational sessions offerred other than the tours. We are experienced travelers and have been on several other cruises with two other companies. We experienced better service, clearer ships, educational sessions and an overall better experience on both of the other lines. Not sure we would try Uniworld again.
