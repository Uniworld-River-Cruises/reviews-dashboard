— Mary, Ganges Voyager II | India's Golden Triangle & the Sacred Ganges | ★★★★★
Only three reasons for deducting one star: 1) While the quality of the food throughout was excellent, there was a very disappointing lack of "Indian" choices in the evenings. Uniworld seemed to assume that all guests are boring and unadventurous, and that they all just want familiar "western" dishes. We met several others who felt the same as we did, and fortunately the very obliging chef was more than happy every night to prepare, just for a group of us, a special Indian main course! We always left this completely up to him to surprise us, and every night we had something invariably superb. Travellers who want to taste the real India will be badly disappointed with the standard menus on this trip. At the start of the cruise that chef did invite guests to make special requests, and seemed genuinely pleased to oblige, but you will have to ask. 2) Gratuities! This has been a source of embarrassment and awkwardness on all our cruises with other companies, so we were delighted that Uniworld go out of their way to stress that their cruises are fully all-inclusive, and that this includes all gratuities. So it was highly disappointing to be told, on our very first meeting with the tour manager, that although tips are indeed already included, any extra gratuities would be welcome from "those who would like to go the extra mile and show their appreciation". He then went on to outline suggested amounts per person per day for drivers, tour guides etc, and that tips for the crew could be left at the bursar's office. This resulted in much awkward discussion between guests about shall we or shan't we, and an unseemly, embarrassing scramble for cash at the end of the cruise, when everyone felt morally compelled to tip all and sundry. The subject of gratuities should NEVER be mentioned by the tour manager. They are already included - paid for in advance within the price of the cruise. End of story. Fully inclusive should mean just that, and whilst individual guests might make a personal choice to pay extra here and there, they should be allowed to do so quietly, privately, and without the moral thumbscrews being put on other guests. 3) For sun lovers the large "sun deck" of the boat is woefully lacking in sun! It is mostly covered over. The little area at the back that is left open has only 6 sunbeds that are usually over-subscribed, and these catch the sun only when it's in the right direction due to the canopy covering the deck. I have stated the above in the hope these points will be useful to prospective travellers, but I must stress that these three points were literally the only things that detracted from an otherwise absolutely superb experience. The standard of the hotels and cruise staterooms was every bit as good as expected from an "ultra-luxury" cruise company, but their level of service and fastidious attention to cleanliness, safety and welfare surpassed expectations, resulting in nobody (to our knowledge) suffering any digestive issues. Uniworld's itineraries are very cleverly structured: They cover all the cultural, historical and touristy essentials, with excellent and knowledgeable tour guides. But equally important are their guided walks, tuktuk rides and rickshaws into the busiest bustling streets and markets in order to fully immerse their guests into the nitty gritty of everyday life. With all of its contrast between opulent luxury and real abject poverty (the raw reality of which some guests found very difficult), we really did feel we left having fully experienced the "real" India, and not just the tourist trail. Thus we highly recommend this tour. If you can justify the extra cost, go for one of the "Heritage" suites that have curved windows giving both front and side views.
