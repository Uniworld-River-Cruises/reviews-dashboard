The cruise was somewhat disappointing. We had done a similar cruise with another company 11 years ago. Although the itineraries looked almost the same, the detailed itinerary on Uniworld was inferior. On the positive side, the service from the staff was top-notch, the food was good, and this picky eater never had a problem finding something appealing. The cabin was also extra-large for a river ship, although much of the additional space was absorbed by the walk-in closet, the large bathroom (very nice with double sinks and more-than-adequate shower), and a king rather than a queen bed.

Staff: Peter — mentioned by Mok | Castles along the Rhine

During our cruise, we had the pleasure of being served by a number of outstanding crew members who made a lasting impression. Our butler Alex was excellent, as were Christen, Alexsadria, Dada at the front desk, Ivana, and Jay—who is clearly a consummate trainer. We also appreciated the service from Martin, Violeta, and Micro at the bar, Levente, Shira (our tour guide), Remus in both the bar and restaurant, Gabriel (our waiter), and Nedko, who guided us through our wine selections. Martin, Maja, and Wikoria—the twins who greeted us with a smile each day—were a particular highlight. I'm sure I may have missed others, as the entire staff was consistently friendly, professional, and attentive. That said, we were disappointed with the physical ship itself. It did not meet the expectations we’ve come to associate with Uniworld—particularly the lack of additional seating areas and the absence of the signature rear bar. As such, we do not plan to cruise again on this or other “Crystal” vessels. We also noted some inconsistencies in the dining experience. Breakfast service was occasionally slow, and eggs and toast were often undercooked. Lunchtime offerings could use improvement, especially as some meats served were quite tough. However, dinners were consistently very good and much appreciated. Unfortunately, our disembarkation experience was poor. Our bus ride to the Brussels airport was a logistical failure. Shortly after leaving the ship, it became clear we were headed in the wrong direction. While we initially assumed the driver was taking an alternate route to the highway, we quickly realized he was unfamiliar with the area. He repeatedly attempted to access a road that was closed to buses and trucks on weekends. After going in circles, we ultimately had to call the cruise director and arrange for taxis to get us to the airport. This last-minute scramble was avoidable and stressful—and it cost us 40 euros out-of-pocket for the cab ride. I trust that Uniworld will reimburse this amount. Despite the shortcomings, the staff made the cruise memorable, and we are grateful for their efforts.
