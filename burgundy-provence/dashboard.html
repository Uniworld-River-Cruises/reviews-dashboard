UniWorld Rhône River Cruise Review: Arles to Lyon Overview of My Experience I am pleased to share my thoughts on my recent Rhône River Cruise with UniWorld, traveling from Arles to Lyon, France. For the most part, this cruise was a positive experience and, as my second voyage with UniWorld—my first being several years ago along Portugal's Douro River—I was able to compare them meaningfully. The ship itself was beautifully appointed and offered a comfortable journey. Areas for Improvement However, there were a few issues that impacted my overall satisfaction. Most noticeably, the absence of a pool on the upper deck diminished the appeal of spending time outdoors. The only available pool was indoors, but it was very small, lacked heat, and was generally unappealing. Flight Assistance Incident A more significant issue arose related to support with our Lufthansa flight from Lyon to Munich. When a complex issue with our flight booking required attention, I requested help from the UniWorld front desk. The staff member connected me to what they believed was the correct Lufthansa department. Unfortunately, the number dialed led to a scam operation rather than an official representative. During a nearly two-hour call at the front desk, I was told by the caller—posing as a Lufthansa agent—that our flights were cancelled and would need to be rebooked, requiring payment of $2,990 per person with the promise of a refund to follow. The conversation was distressing, as I was misled into believing urgent action was required. Aftermath and Response It was only upon returning home that I realized I had been the victim of a scam. I promptly reported the incident to UniWorld, providing the date, time, and description of the staff member involved. The company stated they would investigate and contact the ship. However, it took over two weeks for UniWorld to respond, ultimately informing me they could not confirm the occurrence of the incident with anyone on board. Given the commotion at the front desk, I found it difficult to believe the event was unremembered by staff. UniWorld offered a $500 credit for a future cruise as compensation. Thankfully, American Express thoroughly investigated and reimbursed us for the loss. Final Thoughts While the intentions of the UniWorld representative were good, the mistake led to significant stress and many hours of follow-up after the cruise. This unfortunate incident marred what was otherwise a wonderful travel experience.
