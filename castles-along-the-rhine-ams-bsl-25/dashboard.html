— Lori, Castles Along the Rhine (AMS-BSL) 25 | S.S. Antoinette | ★★★★★ | staff, food, accommodation, wine-drinks
This was our second cruise with Uniworld, both were organized by a winery and all the passengers were part of this group, which I think makes our experience unique. Having said that, the Uniworld experience, from accommodations to food and crew were all wonderful. There are two areas we think could benefit from some improvement. First, the food choices and quality were fantastic, but on our cruise, it seemed the proteins were lacking any seasoning. We did mention this in a survey while still on the cruise, and it seemed to improve before the cruise ended, which was appreciated. The second was temperature control. We did experience an unusual heat wave during our cruise, but the dining room and lounge were uncomfortably hot when filled with passengers. There has to be a way to address this situation.
— Virginia, Castles Along the Rhine (AMS-BSL) 25 | S.S. Antoinette | ★★★★★ | staff, food, accommodation, ship, wine-drinks, service
The ports and excursions were all fun, educational, and interesting. The food was largely wonderful, as was the service, especially our cabin attendant and the restaurant servers. Our cruise director, Ric, was engaging, tireless, and visible around the ship. BUT the ship was unable to function well in the unusually hot weather: cabins, restaurant, lounges were all extremely warm. The staff at the concierge desk did not (weren’t allowed?) acknowledge the issue, only promising repeatedly to “let engineering know.” I’m certain that “engineering” knew, as they were also sweltering on the ship. A simple acknowledgement that the a/c system was working as well as it could would have been preferable to the excuses.
— Jeanne, Castles Along the Rhine (AMS-BSL) 25 | S.S. Antoinette | ★★★ | food-quality, excursion-quality
The ship was going on 15 years of age and lacked a lot of technology updates you would expect - such as usb & power outlets. Wi-Fi connectivity was quite bad as well. The suite wall coverings and curtains were quite dated so much so that the curtains no longer would cover the windows fully letting in more light than you should expect. We had unseasonably hot weather during most of the cruise which made the ship quite hot and the air conditioning was not able to maintain anything close to cool in rooms, dining or gathering areas. When we mentioned that our suite was hot, we were told that engineering would check it out. Got frustrated with receiving that response numerous times when we asked about the heat in the areas. Uniworld would have served themselves much better by telling everyone that the air conditioning on the ship was going to have difficulty keeping up given the extreme heat. We would have appreciated an honest answer of the issue versus being told we would have engineering look at it when, in fact, they knew what the issue was. The food was fine and service was very good. I definitely would not do another Uniworld cruise on this ship (S S Antoinette) if it was the one offered, so it would have to be on one of their newer ships before I would sail them again.
