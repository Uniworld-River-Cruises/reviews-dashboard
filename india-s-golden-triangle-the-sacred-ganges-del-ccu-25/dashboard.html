Overall the trip (land and river) was ok, but.... there are some down points to it: 1. The brochure that was presented to us (at the time of booking) was... well,... 5 years old and not up to date. We did not get to see some major objectives as we hoped to (and paid for) i. e. The Red fort in Jaipur, advertised but not seen! 2. because of the schedule issues, we got to see some 2nd hand objectives, like marble factory, some unsignificant small villages or rice fields, and a lot of road side garbage deposits, that were just fill-up. 3. several issues were encountered when on our "Free-time" we would struggle to obtain restaurant reservations @ locations, India is not a place were a tourist will venture out to eat street food or some sort of local restaurant. 4. SAFETY, this one was a big issue, while on the trip, there where an instance where we had to cross on foot, a railway crossing WITH the BARIERR down, but our local guide decided to cross it anyway despite our concerns, in the group there were elder persons (majority of them) and that presented a REAL safety concerns, making us refuse to attend the next objective. The local guide was oblivion to our expressed concerns. We understood that India is what it is, but... we are not from there, we are coming from countries where safety is a practice not an adventure. 5. Commuting, well,... we spent sometimes more on the road than in the objective (5-6 hours driving for about 160Km /trip...?). I strongly believe that this is an organizational failure and it could be mitigated differently, logistically a nightmare, India is not a country to drive through it, isn't it? The same applies to the transfer to and from the ship, the "jetty" transfer was logistically a disaster, we spent hours for the bus to turn "right" or "left", once we had actually walked to the boat not wanted to spend time for the transfer. The GOD things: local guides were knowledgeable, venues and hotels were excellent, the cruise ship was old but well maintained and the crew EXCELENT, funny and most of all very helpful, concerned for our security, congratulations to the Capitan. Food was good, but on the boat the Chef Mayuk and his crew was above and beyond to accommodate us. All in all a decent trip, not as high on standards as the one on the Nile (EGYPT), but decent. The concerns that we had will probably make us to move for a while to other similar CME conferences/trips. Sorry!
