Wonderful experience from beginning to end!
— Roseanne, Splendors of Egypt & the Nile | River Tosca, S.S. Sphinx | ★★★★★ | overall
The Jordan pre-cruise tour and the River Nile cruise were both excellent. The guides were knowledgeable and informative. Although in Jordan, we did feel like several of the stops were a high pressure sales opportunity, especially the mosaic workshop, and even wondered if the guide had a financial interest in sales made there. We liked the itinerary for Egypt but would have preferred more time in Wadi Rum and Petra on the precruise tour. In Wadi Rum, we arrived just before dinner and left immediately after breakfast, which meant we didn’t really have any daylight to explore or enjoy the surroundings. The day we went to Petra had a lot of driving, making it seem like the time in Petra was very compressed. In Egypt, we enjoyed all of the excursions and Mohamed was a superb guide. However, were a disappointed that so many excursions (“masterpiece experiences”) were not included in the cruise price. It seemed that for an all inclusive fare, it really didn’t live up to the brand promise. The evening entertainment and the regional food added to the experience. The ship staff were all very professional and friendly.

We first did the Jordan extension. It was an interesting experience. Loved the itinary, especially Wadi Rum, Petra, and Aqaba. There were, however very long bus trips without a proper meal breaks. A number of days we had to choose packaged snacks from a petrol station. Our guide was knowledgeable and friendly, but also very opinionated and at times, we felt like we were being lectured. The 12 night Cairo and Nile cruise was wonderful. Our only complaints were the meats and shellfish were overcooked and there weren’t enough activities on the ship., especially in the afternoons. Overall, it was not up to the usual Uni World standards. The staff was friendly and professional. The ship was clean and pretty. The main lounge furniture was very uncomfortable. We also did the Sharm e Sheik post extension. The resort was absolutely beautiful! Our biggest problem was that we went with our guide to a seafood restaurant on the city night tour. After being extremely careful with what we ate and drank during our entire trip, we trusted our uni World guide, which was a hugh mistake. My husband experienced nausea and diarrhea for the next 5 days. He then was hospitalized for 5 days with an extreme bacterial infection and was on sepsis protocol. The infectious disease doctors feel it was caused by the shrimp he ate that night. Although Egypt was wonderful, our experience makes us question future travel with UniWorld.
