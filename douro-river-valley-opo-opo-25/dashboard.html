The ship staff were all excellent. I didn’t enjoy a lot of the tours. I understand the weather changed some plans, but some of the tours were not good. We had a guide that loved embellishing every fact with blood and gore, beheadings, skinning alive on and on. In the bus he started talking about his childhood, at length, no one cared. The worst was when he talked about lambs brains being a delicacy. Cramming all the brains into a glass tank at the butcher’s and the brains getting stuck on the glass. That was when I was compelled to yell out for him to stop! I have never had to behave that way, but it was so gross and graphic. On the same tour, we were given 45 minutes to shop. Shop at the same stores that have the same merchandise at every single store. When we walked around the corner to the bus, there was a beautiful big church and beautiful gardens. I asked him why he didn’t tell us about this area, some of us didn’t have any interest in shopping and I was shocked to see this beautiful attraction that we could have enjoyed. His answer was, he wanted to shock us! He was our guide twice and he really soured our experience. I want to reiterate that the staff on the ship were really fantastic! The ship was very, very nice. We really enjoyed the entertainment, especially the local group of singers, many of whom were family members. I hadn’t laughed that much in a long time. The pastry chef prepared a beautiful and delicious Challah bread for us so the Jewish passengers could celebrate our Sabbath. The staff was very nice to do that!

— Georgina, Douro River Valley (OPO-OPO) 25 | S.S. Sao Gabriel | ★★★★★ | food, service
The S. S. Sao Gabriel is a beautiful ship with great facilities and very freindly crew. I really enjoyed the one week cruise. I recognize that this was the first cruise of the seaon and all the staff were working out some of the things that can only be done once you have guests on board. They were apologetic for any errors and acted upon requests and recommendations promptly! The most significant criticism I have was with the menu. The food was almost always too salty. In fact there were several selections that I could not finish due to the salt. I recognize that certain cultural dishes would have been created with saltfish, but the mushroom soup should have been edible. The pastry chef, Lilliana was a delight. I loved her live cooking demonstration! I also loved the delicious foods she made every day. I appreciated the numbering system for identifcation of foods with potential allergens. I did have to ask about several items on the buffet tables though as not everything had a label. I do not have the risk of anaphylaxis, but for those who are vulnerable it would be essential. On another cruiseline the passengers with food allergies have a private meeting with the chef and Maitre D to confirm the foods to avoid. Each waiter is aware of the guests in question and are very careful to check with the chef when there is any question about an item on the menu. Overall these were minor issues and I am happy to recommend Uniworld River Cruises to anyone!
