We were on 3 different boats. I received only one feedback email which I thought was just for the Catherine. Your review system is confusing. So I did not review the Bon Voyage or the Joie de Vivre. On the Bon Voyage, the cooking class was a lot of fun and we really enjoyed it. The chef gave us a website with the recipes but it is impossible to get into so we have not been able to access them. The cooking class on the Catherine, not so much, in fact, not at all. The chef would not share recipes and all we did was chop vegetables and the food was not that good, especially for the price. Also, on the BV, the excursions were really good, the staff, excellent. Then on to the JDV: that was the least enjoyable cruise. Everything got messed up because of Bastille Day, which happens every year at the same day so why was it such a surprise! Martin and his wife, Stephanie, were the guides and we avoided them after one experience but they were with us for 3 days. They stood around a lot and talked about things that were not of interest. Martin, especially, would get off topic frequently and go down rabbit holes. We were given 15 minutes to go take pictures outside of Norte Dame. It was a 7 minute walk there. This was inexcusable. There was only an 8 minute wait to get in but of course we had not time since Martin had us standing for a very long time just watching the flyover, which was very good. We had been on this cruise in 2017 and went to Giverny early in the morning and there were no crowds and it was lovely. This time it was after 1:00 and hot and crowded. That excursion should absolutely be done early! The wait to get into Monet’s house was 45 minutes so we skipped it, having done it before. This was not a pleasant experience. On to the Catherine. This boat was lovely and Ivelin our butler, was a joy. We really liked the little table and 2 chairs that were in the suite, and we only had dock views one day so the room was very enjoyable. The cooking class and the kayak tour overlapped so you could not do both. Cooking class was a bust as described above. One reason we have gone on only Uniworld river cruses is because, in the past, everything was included. We were willing to pay more for the cruise so we didn’t have to be nickeled and dimed during the cruise. Now that Uniworld has been sold to a corporation it seems the accountants have taken over, as often happens. Where did those Masterpiece excursions come from?? They used to be included. I know, because we have done this exact cruise in 2017. It was wonderful and this one was totally different and not so good. People, don’t let the accountants ruin Uniworld! Bea would be very unhappy if she knew this was happening. I really doubt we will cruise with Uniworld again.

— Darren, Ultimate France | S.S. Bon Voyage | ★★★ | general-dissatisfaction
The food was very good…..the service in the dining room & lounge was excellent. We had wait staff who remembered our names & remembered what we lied to drink or how we liked our coffee or tea. On several occasions, when we had transfers to another ship, or at the airport, we had to drag luggage with us. There was no one to help. On one occasion, we had to sit on a bus for an hour to wait for additional at a hotel in Paris A second transport would have been nice. We were left off the list for the shore excursion to the Queens Hamlet. The tour manager, Pierre, blamed it on the reception staff & initially said that he was sorry, but we could not go. After some discussion, he agreed to get us there by taxi, & went with us ( a very expensive taxi ride paid for by Uniworld). On arrival we were to meet up with the Uniworld group, but we were not allowed to use our headsets because the venue employees would know that the group had exceeded the limit for group size. This would endanger the license of the local guide. A day later we received a bottle of sparkling wine along with a note of apology. This was a bit redundant since, in a suite, we could have ordered this bottle of wine any day of the cruise. Perhaps a more appropriate gesture would have been a credit for whatever Masterpiece excursions were available. One of the high points of this cruise was our butler, Dino. He was there whenever we needed anything, although, it was unusual that we needed, or wanted anything, because he anticipated our wants & needs before we did. He always had a smile on his face, & really seemed to enjoy his work. Uniworld is fortunate to have him as an employee! A few days before our cruise ended, our ship lost its anchor. This was an inconvenience because we had longer bus rides for the excursions, & to the airport. We were never told why this anchor was lost………was it incompetence, poor maintenance, or just a freak accident. This was our first Uniworld trip, & likely will be our last. We just did not feel that the excursions & local guides were as good as they could have been. There was not enough attention paid to detail on occasion.
— Nancy, Ultimate France | S.S. Bon Voyage | ★★★ | food-quality, ship-condition, excursion-quality, value
