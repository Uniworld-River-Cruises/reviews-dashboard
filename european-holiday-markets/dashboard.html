— Joanne, European Holiday Markets | S.S. Beatrice, River Princess | ★★★★★ | staff, excursions, service
Our embarkation experience was smooth, and we appreciated having access to the lounge until our stateroom was ready. However, several aspects of the ship and itinerary fell short of expectations. Ship & Accommodation While the ship was attractively decorated, the garland on the stair handrails created challenges for guests with limited mobility. Our cabin was significantly smaller than anticipated, with a large gap around the door that allowed in both noise and light. The chair and table beside the bed were not functional and posed a tripping hazard. The bathroom layout was particularly problematic; the placement of the toilet paper holder caused repeated injury, and housekeeping twice serviced the room without replenishing toilet paper. With only one lounge on board and limited seating in the cabin, there was no quiet space to retreat from noise. Food and beverage service was also disappointing. The number of buffets was unexpected, and the lack of regional dishes contradicted what had been advertised. Non‑alcoholic beverage options were limited, and I was served Coke Zero without being informed. Drink refills at dinner were inconsistent unless ordering wine, and repeated requests for iced tea refills went unanswered. Port Experiences Travel into Nuremberg was easy, but the return to the ship in Roth was disorganized. The shuttle bus arrived 45 minutes late, could not accommodate all waiting guests, and no alternative transportation was offered. The stop in Regensburg was underwhelming; both the morning and evening markets were extremely small. Our guide did not bring a microphone, and once at the market, no meeting point or return instructions were provided. Passau was enjoyable, though the audio devices and heavy winter gear were unnecessary since most of the tour was indoors. It would have been helpful to know in advance that the market was cash‑only. We were unable to visit Linz due to a propeller issue, reportedly caused by the ship striking something earlier in the journey. Although we were allowed off the ship briefly, the delay significantly impacted the schedule. Many guests expressed that they would have preferred visiting Linz over the brief stop in Dürnstein. In Vienna, the morning excursion options were presented as a combination of the Belvedere and the market, but in reality, there was only enough time for one. The excursion was scheduled until 12:30, yet we returned to the ship shortly after noon. The optional Schönbrunn Palace tour also felt rushed, with insufficient time for both the tour and shopping. Departure Despite indicating a 10:00 a.m. departure on the required form, we were instructed to vacate our room by 8:30 a.m., with breakfast ending at 8:00. Our request to adjust the taxi time was denied, and no additional assistance was offered. Service Communication throughout the trip was inconsistent, and on several occasions, staff appeared unhelpful or dismissive. Christian, in particular, often made guests feel as though asking questions was an inconvenience. The only standout service we experienced was from our guide Chris in Passau, who was exceptional. This was our first Uniworld cruise, and based on this experience, we are uncertain whether we would book with the company again. Several returning guests shared similar concerns, noting that this trip did not align with their past experiences with Uniworld.
