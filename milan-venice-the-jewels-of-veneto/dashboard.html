— Irvin, Milan, Venice & the Jewels of Veneto | S.S. La Venezia | ★★★ | general-dissatisfaction
This was my first cruise with Uniworld, and as an advertised luxury cruise line, I have to say I was not very impressed. The ship itself felt dated. The rooms were extremely small, and the balloon blinds in the cabins covered half of the windows—frustrating, since I had paid extra for the view. On the positive side, the beds were very comfortable. I also felt the bar with loud entertainment was poorly located, right above staterooms which meant entertainment often impacted guests who were trying to sleep. The excursions were interesting but rushed, leaving little free time to explore and enjoy the beautiful places we visited. WiFi was very spotty and often unavailable, which is unacceptable these days, especially on a luxury cruise line. The food was average at best. I enjoy seafood, but only local fresh fish was offered once; otherwise, farmed fish dominated the menu. On buffet night, I picked up a dirty plate—only to find several more dirty ones underneath. This was disappointing and not what I’d expect from a luxury experience. Most of the staff were friendly, but many seemed undertrained compared to other luxury hotels and cruise lines I’ve experienced. Overall, while there were some positives, I don’t feel the experience lived up to the “luxury” branding. At this point, I’m not sure I could recommend Uniworld or book another cruise with them in the future.
