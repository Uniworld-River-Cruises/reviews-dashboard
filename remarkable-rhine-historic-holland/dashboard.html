This was our first Uniworld river cruise, and we were very pleased: with the accommodations, the food service, the responsiveness of the staff, and the included excursions. Two comments: First, we had to ask about the distance traveled up the Rhine River. On the last evening, we stopped the captain and he told us. Why was this such a secret? Second, we are land lubbers so anything related to water travel is fascinating to us. Thus, I was very interested in the history and development of the Rhine River for such a level of travel. In addition, an explanation of the 12 locks we traversed would have made for an interesting chat with our Cruise Director. We learned on the last evening that this was his first cruise, and I think that he did a great job! We had a French Canadian group onboard, and I think that coordinating with that cruise director in advance might have improved some of the excursion departures. Last, departures for some of the excursions was a little confusing among the specific Jewish Heritage tours, the regular tours and the "slower than normal" tours. No major inconveniences, but just a note that the guidance about where to go and when could have been a bit better. Hopefully, we'll be able to enjoy another Uniworld river cruise in the future!
