Great pre-trip to Biarritz and the 5-star Hotel du Palis, set high expectations. Boarding the boat, less so ... confusion after initial desk sign-in/photo taking and sending us to lunch -- at lunch an announcement was made that rooms were ready. Rooms were unlocked, so we unpacked. No one came by, so we went to reception to get key cards, only to be scolded (yes scolded) for not coming to desk from lunch for the key. (No one at lunch said to go to the desk). The boat is old -- most decks (inc lounge and restaurant floors) are old teak planks showing dirt/wear, the brass bar counter in the lounge was neither polished or clean, rugs in the state room showed wear and stains. Rooms incredibly small and difficult to navigate. Food was inconsistent, with only a couple of entrees rating a "this was very good" and lunch buffets generally iffy. Excursions were acceptable; venues visited very nice (cooking class was great but did not deliver promised receipes), and guides were very good. The rivers are a constant mud brown (crew called it chocolate milk) from silt, not Uniworld's fault, but an unexpected feature which did not help overcome the other issues. Transfer to Paris for the post-trip was flawless, and the Hotel du Louve is the quality expected of Uniworld, except that breakfast buffet was small for a Hyatt-affiliated property with consistent watery scrambled eggs. We were not alone in overall disappointment -- past Uniworld guests with multiple cruises were disappointed and it was a frequent topic of discussion.
