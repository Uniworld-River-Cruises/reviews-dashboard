This experience did not meet Uniworld's advertising nor our expectations. Comparatively, our previous Christmas markets cruise aboard Scenic on the Danube was far superior in many respects. First, please do not waste my time with your bogus bidding for superior accommodations scheme. My time is valuable, and you wasted my time with this marketing ploy when the superior suites were not available. Second, please do not advertise that butlers are available for each suite when your butlers are spread and shared among all the suites, such that they physically cannot afford the time to accommodate guests appropriately in each of the suites (as an examply, Yuri greeted us on arrival, but had no time to orient us to the controls in our suite for television, windows, shades, or climate control). Third, please do not advertise that foods are locally sourced and carefully curated when we observed delivery of frozen and refrigerated food stuffs from wholesalers at dockside. Your food (choices, portions and quality of cuts of meat and fish) was mediocre. Ask our waiter Marco: my bride chose chicken and french fries for dinner each evening. Fourth, our suite (no. 403 on S.S. Antoinette) had repeated issues with a) heating as engineers were in our suite four separate times resetting the fuse for the furnace to function properly, b) one of the florets on the mirror adjacent to the port side of the bed was broken so that it was unsightly and perhaps even a safety hazard, 3) the sound system for the television was impaired (it acts like the speakers are being turned on and off each time a character emits sound) which was so annoying we left the system on silent, and 4) there was no closed captioning available for the entertainment options which were available on the suite's television. Fifth, we had one good local guide in Cologne. The rest were at best marginal. The lady on the bus to Lucerne was as interesting as dry, unbuttered toast. The lady from the Basque region of Spain in Baden Baden was tolerable for about five minutes, but then we had to walk away on our own. Sixth, we were rushed during our short stay in Strasbourg, the bus ride there was entirely too long, and as the vessel was moved closer while we were there (so that we would not be confronted with a two-hour return trip) we should have been given more time on the ground to enjoy Strasbourg. Seventh, please don't advertise the cruise as a Christmas markets cruise starting in Basel when there were no - zero - Christmas markets open in Basel when we arrived prior to embarkation or during our first night aboard your vessel. Finally, if true that Uniworld is now owned by a private equity group, please just tell the truth so consumers can make informed choices. It seems like food selection, maintenance and staffing issues are now governed by cost issues and profit margins, not stellar service as advertised. Bottom line: we didn't get what we paid for.
