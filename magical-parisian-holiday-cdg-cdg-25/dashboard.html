The staff were very good. The food was average and unadventurous, sometimes cold when delivered to the table. The Steak was inedible as commented by many guests. Coffee and Tea was never hot, mainly because the water was being kept hot in a vacuum flask not freshly boiled. Not nearly as good as previous Uniworld trips. Our cabin (a suite) was disappointing - the wall lights either side of the bed didn't work from day 1 and then the whole time we were there, a picture light was the same, the television didn't work either. There was only one reading light on the wall beside the bed. We couldn't get any of these defects repaired, a problem experienced by other guests. The relevant staff on the ship must have known about the cabin defects as they were there on our arrival. In the main, the excursions onshore resulted in long coach journeys which were too long for what was offered at the destination. As some American friends said - more bucks, less bang! The trips that required an extra cost were vastly overpriced, in our opinion. Virtually every move on the river was carried out at night so no chance to see any scenery. Uniworld boasts to be one of the best River Cruising firms, however, on this accasion they weren't.
